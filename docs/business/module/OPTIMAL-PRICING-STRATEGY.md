## Pricing Psychology

### Principle 1: Charm Pricing (Ending in 9)

**Why it works:**
- Numbers ending in 9 are perceived as significantly lower
- "99,000" feels much cheaper than "100,000"
- Reduces "pain of paying" psychologically

**Application:**

| Instead of | Use | Perception Difference |
|------------|-----|----------------------|
| IDR 500,000 | **IDR 499,000** | Feels like "400s" not "500s" |
| IDR 100,000 | **IDR 99,000** | "Under 100k" psychological barrier |
| IDR 200,000 | **IDR 199,000** | Anchors to 100s range |
| IDR 400,000 | **IDR 399,000** | "300-something" vs "400" |

**Evidence:**
- MIT study: $39 products sell 24% more than $40 products
- ".99" pricing increases sales by 8-15% on average
- Works even when customers know it's a trick!

### Van Westendorp Price Sensitivity Meter

Survey customers (pre-launch):

**Questions:**
1. At what price would this module be so expensive you wouldn't consider it? (Too Expensive)
2. At what price would you consider it expensive, but still consider it? (Expensive)
3. At what price would you consider it a bargain? (Cheap)
4. At what price would it be so cheap you'd question the quality? (Too Cheap)

**Analysis:**
```
Plot the 4 curves, find:
- Optimal Price Point (OPP): Intersection of "Expensive" and "Cheap"
- Range of Acceptable Prices: Between "Too Cheap" and "Too Expensive"
```
